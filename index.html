Project idea

Project idea: My project idea is a simple one: make an automatic puppy feeder! Yes plenty of them exist but I want one built especially for my house, one that is not similar to Doc Brown’s can opener in “Back to the future”, one that instead has food stored in it, and is set to a timer, so all the machine has to do is turn a motor, releasing the food onto a set location. Motivation My motivation is that I own a puppy myself, her eating habits are poor and all over the place. She often doesn't eat enough because there is no one there to give it to her, and she sometimes eats too much. Having the automatic food dispenser would solve both of these problems. Automation of physical tasks especially with pets has been on the rise in recent years, so it would be a great opportunity to work on something like this. Description The automatic dog feeder itself would be a small device that could be placed anywhere in the house, its portability would mean the use of the machine could be anywhere within a house or outside. The machine would be made from a raspberry pi, a motor, and the appropriate plastic models, including a scoop/storage unit holding food, that would be flipped over by the motor, and the motor would be activated by the raspberry pi, timed every so many hours. The size of the machine would not have to be big at all, in fact should only be able to serve on cup of dog food, meaning that the motor should only be able to spin about 300 grams (serving cup included) which means the motor(s) will only need to produce 3 newtons of force. The raspberry pi program would be easy to make from scratch but could also be copied online, all it would require is a timer that is to be activated once turned on, and a certain voltage administered to the motor once the timer ends. The effect would be a controlled and timed diet for a puppy, meaning the product could be used when owners are either home, or away. An important note is that the product should not supplement human nourishment and a full eating plan and should not be used in that way. The device is simply trying to take out the manual labour of putting food into a bowl. Another aspect of the device could be a sensor / button that the puppy can be trained to use when it is hungry, however this feature might not be implemented due to ethical reasons and whether it goes against the purpose of the product’s aim, to regulate the puppy’s diet. The competitors of this product mostly include stations that are pre set and not entirely programmable, my product would stand out from these devices because it would not be an entire unit, ie the bowl and food holder, it would be entirely portable and not as bulky. The price range of these product is usually set at $100 and my product would cost not even half of that as the materiels (raspberry pi, plastic scoops, DC motor and holding set) are not overly priced and designed to be sold at a retail level. Skills required: The ability to create some sort of code or modify it to make the timer, and motor command function is essential, as well as the ability to create a holder that would act as a chassis for the raspberry pi, motor, wires and food scoops. The ability to make the product as portable, and as neat as possible is preferred. Time management skills will also be required for making this device. Knowledge of the simple physics involved with the device is also required and consideration of the aim of the device is needed to be kept in mind. Outcome: The aim of the device is simply to remove the manual labour of putting pet food into a bowl every so often, so when the device is able to effectively perform that task, it would have been successful. It will need to be consistent in performing the task without major errors. If the device encounters design problems they can be fixed and the design can be altered as long as the original idea and aim is achieved, if programming and motor problems occur, there may need to be a revision of the systems in place to perform the aim.
